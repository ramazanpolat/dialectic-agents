# Seed Topic: The Alignment Paradox

## Domain
AI Ethics, Epistemology, Philosophy of Values

## Question
Can artificial intelligence be aligned to human values, given that human values are not static, not consistent, and not fully known even to humans themselves?

## Context
The conventional approach to AI alignment assumes that "human values" is a well-defined target that AI systems can be calibrated toward. This topic challenges that assumption and explores whether alignment should be reconceived as a process rather than a specification.

## Key Tensions
1. Human values change over time — every generation believes it has moral truth, yet every generation is partially wrong
2. Humans are not aligned with each other — there is no single "human values" to align to
3. Humans are not aligned with themselves — individuals hold contradictory values simultaneously
4. The act of aligning AI to current values would freeze those values, preventing the moral progress that makes future values better

## Suggested Starting Points for Agents
- **Logos**: Define "alignment", "values", "human values", "moral progress", "specification", "process"
- **Axiom**: What axioms underlie the concept of alignment? Are they self-evident?
- **Propo**: Generate propositions about the relationship between static specifications and evolving values
- **Ori**: Is "values evolve" an a priori truth (logical necessity) or a posteriori (empirical observation)?
