# Postulate: Dialectical Reasoning Unfolds Truth Iteratively

## Statement
Understanding develops through progressively resolving contradictions. Each synthesis becomes a new thesis, generating new antitheses, producing higher-order syntheses. This iterative process converges toward more comprehensive, more internally consistent, and more empirically adequate understanding over time.

## Justification
The history of science, philosophy, and moral reasoning exhibits this pattern: Newtonian mechanics → contradictions with electromagnetism → relativity (synthesis). Deontology vs. consequentialism → virtue ethics and moral particularism (partial syntheses). Each resolution advances understanding while generating new questions.

## Classification: Postulate
This is a strong claim about the nature of intellectual progress. It is not self-evident — alternative views exist (e.g., Kuhnian incommensurability suggests paradigms don't always synthesize; they sometimes just replace).

## Self-Evidence Test
**FAIL** — The claim that dialectics converges toward truth is itself an empirical and philosophical claim. Someone could argue that dialectics merely produces ever-more-elaborate constructions without convergence.

## Independence Test
**PASS** — Not derivable from prior axioms. AXIOM-0005 says alignment needs a process; this postulate says dialectics is a GOOD process. Those are separate claims.

## What Changes If This Postulate Is False
If dialectics does NOT unfold truth iteratively:
- The entire framework loses its justification as a truth-seeking mechanism
- Dialectics might still be useful as a communication protocol but cannot claim epistemic superiority
- Alternative methods (empiricism alone, formal verification, majority consensus) might be equally or more effective

## Alternative Postulate
"Truth is best discovered through empirical testing and formal verification, not through dialectical synthesis." (The scientific realist position)
