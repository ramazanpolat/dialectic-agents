# Postulate: Natural Selection Optimized for Survival, Not Truth

## Statement
The human cognitive architecture was shaped by evolutionary pressures that selected for reproductive fitness, not for accurate representation of reality. Human perception, reasoning, and intuition are therefore systematically biased in ways that served ancestral survival but do not reliably track truth.

## Justification
Cognitive biases (confirmation bias, availability heuristic, in-group favoritism, agency detection, etc.) are not random defects — they are features that provided survival advantages in ancestral environments. A heuristic that's fast and mostly-right outcompetes one that's slow and accurate, in evolutionary terms.

## Classification: Postulate
Domain-specific to human cognition. Supported by extensive empirical evidence from evolutionary psychology and cognitive science but not purely self-evident.

## Self-Evidence Test
**FAIL as self-evident** — Conceivable that selection pressures could have produced truth-tracking cognition. The postulate requires empirical support.

## Independence Test
**PASS** — Not derivable from the other axioms, though it provides explanatory grounding for AXIOM-0004 (why humans are misaligned with themselves).

## What Changes If This Postulate Is False
If human cognition DOES reliably track truth, then:
- Cognitive biases are anomalies, not features
- Introspection can be trusted
- The metacognitive bugs are fixable through education alone, without requiring external scaffolding (like AI partners)
