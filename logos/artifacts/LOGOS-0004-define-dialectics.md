# Definition: Dialectics

## Statement
**Dialectics** (noun): A method of reasoning that advances understanding through the structured confrontation and resolution of opposing positions (thesis and antithesis) into a higher-order synthesis that preserves the valid elements of each while transcending their contradiction.

## Justification
Dialectics is the core protocol of this entire system. The definition must capture that it is a METHOD (not a belief), that it is STRUCTURED (not mere debate), and that synthesis is TRANSCENDENCE (not compromise).

## Genus and Differentia
- **Genus**: A method of reasoning
- **Differentia**: Distinguished from debate by requiring synthesis (not winning); distinguished from compromise by requiring transcendence (not splitting the difference); distinguished from analysis by requiring opposition (not decomposition)

## Positive Example
Thesis: "Free will exists." Antithesis: "All events are determined." Synthesis: "Agents can act on reasons even within a deterministic causal framework" (compatibilism) — this preserves the valid core of both positions.

## Negative Example
"Some people believe in free will and some don't, and both views have merit" is NOT dialectics — it's relativism. No synthesis occurred; the contradiction was merely acknowledged.
