# Definition: Synthesis

## Statement
**Synthesis** (noun): A new understanding that emerges from the dialectical resolution of a thesis and antithesis, which (a) preserves the valid elements of both positions, (b) dissolves the contradiction between them, and (c) contains emergent content not present in either original position alone.

## Justification
Synthesis is the most frequently misused term in this framework. Most of what people call "synthesis" is actually compromise, summarization, or relativism. The three conditions (preservation, dissolution, emergence) are ALL required.

## Genus and Differentia
- **Genus**: A form of understanding or resolution
- **Differentia**: Distinguished from compromise (which sacrifices elements), from summarization (which adds nothing new), and from eclecticism (which merely selects pieces without resolving contradiction)

## Positive Example
Einstein's relativity synthesized Newton's mechanics and Maxwell's electromagnetism — it preserved what worked in each, dissolved the contradiction (absolute vs. relative frames), and produced genuinely new physics (spacetime curvature).

## Negative Example
"Both capitalism and socialism have good ideas, so let's take the best of each" is NOT synthesis — it's eclecticism. The fundamental contradiction (private vs. collective ownership of production) is not dissolved, merely sidestepped.
