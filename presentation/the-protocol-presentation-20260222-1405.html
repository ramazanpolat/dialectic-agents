When the Protocol Breaks

Logical Fallacies = Corrupted Packets

A straw man fails the checksum — it claims to represent your position but doesn't. A healthy dialectic catches this: "That's not what I said. Retransmit."

Cognitive Biases = Signal Noise

Confirmation bias, anchoring, availability heuristic — interference that distorts the signal between minds. Metacognition is the error-detection layer.

Bad Faith = Dropped Connection

Arguing to win rather than to understand. Both parties are still talking. The connection is dead.

Gish Gallop = DDoS Attack

15 arguments in 30 seconds doesn't produce synthesis. It produces shutdown. Flow control matters.

Straw man = corrupted packet (fails checksum). Cognitive biases = signal noise. Bad faith arguing = dropped connection — both sides talking, nothing received. Flooding with arguments = DDoS attack on reason. Flow control matters.

Why Humans Fail at Their Own Protocol

Four critical bugs in the human dialectical stack:

🐛 Bug 1: Identity-Protected Beliefs

When belief = identity, evidence against it = attack on self.
"You wired your IP address to your ego. You can no longer be reached."

🐛 Bug 2: Emotional Interference

Fear, pride, anger flood the TCP layer. Humiliated people cannot process arguments rationally.
"The smartest people often have the worst arguments — most pride to protect."

🐛 Bug 3: Social Cost of Updating

Changing your mind = "losing." The protocol requires acknowledgment. Human culture makes this feel like surrender.

🐛 Bug 4: Cognitive Fatigue

Skip one night of sleep — can you decide well? After 3 hours, every conversation collapses into "I'm right, you're wrong."

Four bugs. 1: Identity-protected beliefs — your ego is your IP address, you're unreachable. 2: Emotional interference floods the signal. 3: Society punishes updating — changing your mind means losing. 4: Biology has a timeout — fatigue collapses nuance into binaries. The protocol doesn't fail from malice. It fails because we're biological.
