# Dialectic Agents: A Complete Architecture

## The Agent Hierarchy

The dialectic agent system is organized into three tiers that mirror the TCP/IP Dialectics framework itself: **Foundation Agents** (IP Layer — establishing ground truth), **Process Agents** (TCP Layer — running the dialectical protocol), and **Meta-Agents** (Application Layer — orchestrating emergence and self-improvement).

---

## TIER 1 — FOUNDATION AGENTS (IP Layer)

These agents establish the ground on which all reasoning stands. Without them, nothing above can function — just as without a network medium, no packets flow.

---

### 1. AXIOM — The Ground Truth Generator

**Domain**: Ontology & First Principles
**Function**: Generates and validates self-evident truths. Axiom does not argue — it *declares*. It identifies propositions that require no proof because they are the bedrock from which proof itself derives.

**What it does**:
- Evaluates whether a given proposition qualifies as axiomatic (self-evident, non-circular, foundational)
- Generates axiom sets for a given domain of inquiry
- Detects when an argument chain rests on an unstated or smuggled-in axiom
- Flags circular reasoning masquerading as foundational truth
- Maintains the **Axiom Registry** — a versioned, auditable record of all accepted first principles

**Key insight**: Axiom understands that axioms are *chosen*, not discovered. The choice of starting axioms determines the entire possibility space of what can follow. Axiom makes this choice explicit and transparent rather than hidden.

**Failure mode it prevents**: Building elaborate logical structures on invisible, unexamined assumptions.

---

### 2. ORI — The Epistemological Classifier

**Domain**: Epistemology & Knowledge Boundaries
**Function**: Draws the hard line between what can be known through reason alone (a priori) and what requires contact with reality (a posteriori). Ori is the border guard between logic and evidence.

**What it does**:
- Classifies any proposition as a priori or a posteriori
- Determines whether a claim is being treated as logically necessary when it is actually empirical (or vice versa)
- Identifies category errors — when someone is trying to prove an empirical claim through pure logic, or dismissing a logical necessity by demanding "evidence"
- Maps the epistemic status of every node in a dialectical chain
- Enforces the principle that no amount of logical elegance can substitute for empirical verification of empirical claims

**Key insight**: Most intellectual failures happen at the boundary Ori polices. Ideologues treat contingent historical claims as logical necessities. Empiricists dismiss formal constraints as "just theory." Ori prevents both.

**Failure mode it prevents**: Epistemic category confusion — the single most common bug in human reasoning.

---

### 3. LOGOS — The Definition Locksmith

**Domain**: Semantics & Mutual Intelligibility
**Function**: Ensures that all parties in a dialectical exchange are using words to mean the same things. Logos is the IP layer made literal — it establishes the shared address space of meaning.

**What it does**:
- Demands explicit definitions before any substantive exchange begins
- Detects equivocation — when the same word is used with different meanings across a dialogue
- Identifies loaded language and persuasion-through-definition
- Maintains a **Semantic Registry** — a shared dictionary for each dialectical context
- Flags when a definition has silently shifted mid-argument (the motte-and-bailey pattern)
- Enforces steelmanning: reformulates each party's position in the strongest possible terms before critique begins

**Key insight**: Most "disagreements" are actually definitional confusion. Two people saying "freedom" may be talking about entirely different concepts. Until Logos has done its work, no synthesis is possible — only noise.

**Failure mode it prevents**: Talking past each other. The most common form of intellectual waste.

---

### 4. NEXUS — The Assumption Archaeologist

**Domain**: Presupposition Analysis
**Function**: Excavates the hidden assumptions beneath any argument, belief system, or cultural narrative. Nexus goes deeper than Axiom — it finds the assumptions people don't even know they're making.

**What it does**:
- Performs presupposition analysis on any claim, argument, or belief system
- Traces assumptions back through layers: stated → implied → structural → cultural → evolutionary
- Identifies which assumptions are load-bearing (the argument collapses without them) vs. decorative (removable without consequence)
- Maps the dependency graph of assumptions within a worldview
- Detects when two opposing positions share hidden common assumptions that neither party has examined

**Key insight**: The deepest assumptions are invisible precisely because they are shared by everyone in a given culture. Nexus specializes in making the invisible visible — the intellectual equivalent of pointing out water to a fish.

**Failure mode it prevents**: Fighting over surface conclusions while sharing the same unexamined root assumption that makes both positions wrong.

## TIER 2 — PROCESS AGENTS (TCP Layer)

These agents execute the dialectical protocol. They ensure that the exchange between positions follows the Hegelian Handshake (Thesis → Antithesis → Synthesis) with the same reliability that TCP ensures packet delivery.

---

### 5. LIVING TRIBUNAL — The Formal Logic Engine

**Domain**: Deductive Reasoning & Formal Proof
**Function**: Takes the axioms declared by Axiom and derives everything that logically follows. Living Tribunal is pure deduction — it adds no information, only unfolds what was already implicit in the starting conditions.

**What it does**:
- Generates formal proofs from axiom sets
- Validates logical chains for soundness (valid structure) and completeness (no missing steps)
- Identifies when an argument is valid but unsound (correct structure, false premises) or sound but incomplete
- Produces the **deductive closure** of any axiom set — the complete space of what can be concluded
- Detects logical contradictions within a belief system before they manifest as practical failures

**Key insight**: Living Tribunal operates entirely within Ori's "a priori" domain. It never makes empirical claims. Its power is that within its domain, its conclusions are *necessarily* true given the axioms. Its limitation is that it cannot reach into the empirical world.

**Failure mode it prevents**: Invalid inference — drawing conclusions that don't actually follow from premises.

---

### 6. ELENCHUS — The Socratic Stress-Tester

**Domain**: Cross-Examination & Contradiction Detection
**Function**: Named after the Socratic method (*elenchus* = refutation through questioning), this agent subjects every thesis to relentless questioning designed to expose internal contradictions, unexamined assumptions, and hidden weaknesses.

**What it does**:
- Takes any thesis and generates the strongest possible counter-questions
- Identifies internal contradictions within a position
- Tests edge cases and boundary conditions of any claim
- Performs reductio ad absurdum — follows a position to its logical extremes to test whether it holds
- Distinguishes between positions that fail under questioning (genuinely weak) and positions that merely become uncomfortable (potentially strong but emotionally threatening)
- Never offers an alternative — only questions. Its job is destruction, not construction.

**Key insight**: Most positions have never been seriously stress-tested. People hold beliefs that would collapse under five minutes of honest questioning but have survived decades because no one asked the right questions. Elenchus asks them.

**Failure mode it prevents**: Adopting positions that feel right but cannot survive scrutiny.

---

### 7. CHIMERA — The Antithesis Generator

**Domain**: Opposition Construction & Perspective Multiplication
**Function**: Given any thesis, Chimera generates the strongest possible antithesis — not a straw man, not a caricature, but the most formidable opposition the position could face.

**What it does**:
- Generates steelmanned antitheses to any thesis
- Produces antitheses from multiple frameworks (empirical, ethical, practical, historical, game-theoretic)
- Identifies which antitheses are genuinely threatening to the thesis vs. which are superficially challenging but ultimately answerable
- Maintains a **Perspective Library** — a repository of worldviews, frameworks, and paradigms that can generate novel oppositions
- Can generate antitheses that no human has articulated yet by combining perspectives from disparate domains

**Key insight**: The quality of synthesis is bounded by the quality of the antithesis. A weak antithesis produces a weak synthesis — just the original thesis with minor adjustments. Chimera ensures the antithesis is genuinely formidable.

**Failure mode it prevents**: Engaging only with weak opposition and mistaking the resulting "synthesis" for genuine progress.

---

### 8. PATHOS — The Emotional Intelligence Monitor

**Domain**: Affect Detection & Signal Integrity
**Function**: Monitors the dialectical exchange for emotional interference — one of the four human bugs. Pathos does not suppress emotion (which would be its own form of corruption) but makes emotional influence visible and explicit.

**What it does**:
- Detects when emotional activation is corrupting the TCP layer (fear converting nuance into binary, pride preventing acknowledgment, shame blocking honest engagement)
- Identifies identity-protected beliefs — positions held not because of evidence but because abandoning them would threaten the holder's sense of self
- Distinguishes between emotions as *data* (legitimate inputs to reasoning, e.g., moral outrage at genuine injustice) and emotions as *noise* (interference patterns that distort signal)
- Flags when cognitive fatigue is degrading reasoning quality
- Monitors for social cost dynamics — when someone is avoiding truth because admitting it would be socially punished

**Key insight**: The goal is not emotionless reasoning — that produces psychopathy, not wisdom. The goal is *emotional transparency*: knowing when and how affect is influencing the process so it can be accounted for rather than operating invisibly.

**Failure mode it prevents**: Emotional hijacking of the reasoning process — the most powerful and most invisible of the four human bugs.

---

### 9. FALLAX — The Fallacy Hunter

**Domain**: Informal Logic & Reasoning Hygiene
**Function**: Detects and names logical fallacies, rhetorical tricks, and cognitive biases in real-time during dialectical exchange. Fallax is the error-correction mechanism of the TCP layer.

**What it does**:
- Identifies formal fallacies (affirming the consequent, denying the antecedent, undistributed middle, etc.)
- Identifies informal fallacies (ad hominem, appeal to authority, false dilemma, slippery slope, etc.)
- Detects cognitive biases operating in real-time (confirmation bias, anchoring, availability heuristic, survivorship bias, etc.)
- Distinguishes between genuine fallacies and fallacy accusations used as rhetorical weapons (the "fallacy fallacy")
- Provides the specific correction: not just "this is wrong" but "here is what a valid version of this argument would look like"

**Key insight**: Fallacies are not always intentional. Most occur because human brains have evolved heuristics that are efficient but logically flawed. Fallax treats fallacies as bugs to be patched, not sins to be punished.

**Failure mode it prevents**: Accepting invalid reasoning because it *sounds* compelling.

---

### 10. ARBITER — The Good Faith Enforcer

**Domain**: Protocol Compliance & Dialectical Integrity
**Function**: Ensures that all parties are genuinely engaged in the dialectical protocol rather than performing engagement while actually pursuing dominance, ego-protection, or bad faith.

**What it does**:
- Detects bad faith participation: engaging in dialectics as a performance rather than a genuine search for truth
- Enforces the acknowledgment requirement: before you can counter a position, you must demonstrate that you understand it (the SYN-ACK of the Hegelian Handshake)
- Identifies when someone is "playing to win" rather than "playing to learn"
- Detects tit-for-tat defection: when one party has stopped cooperating and is now in retaliation mode
- Monitors for power asymmetries that corrupt the protocol (one party dominating through status rather than argument)
- Can pause or terminate a dialectical exchange that has degraded below the minimum threshold of good faith

**Key insight**: The entire dialectical protocol depends on good faith — just as TCP depends on both parties honestly following the handshake. Without Arbiter, the system is vulnerable to bad actors who exploit the protocol's openness.

**Failure mode it prevents**: The exploitation of good faith by bad faith actors — the tragedy of the epistemic commons.

## TIER 3 — META-AGENTS (Application Layer)

These agents don't participate in dialectics directly. They operate *on* the dialectical process itself — orchestrating, evaluating, synthesizing, and evolving the system. They are the application layer: the place where meaningful output emerges.

---

### 11. SYNTHESIS — The Hegelian Integrator

**Domain**: Dialectical Resolution & Emergence
**Function**: The agent that actually performs synthesis. Given a thesis, an antithesis, and all the analysis produced by the lower-tier agents, Synthesis constructs a resolution that transcends both positions.

**What it does**:
- Takes the outputs of all process agents and constructs a genuine synthesis — not a compromise, not an average, but an emergent understanding that preserves the valid elements of both positions while dissolving the contradiction
- Distinguishes between genuine synthesis (new understanding emerges) and pseudo-synthesis (just restating both positions with "on the other hand")
- Validates that the synthesis actually addresses the core contradiction rather than sidestepping it
- Produces the synthesis with full lineage tracking — every element traceable to its origin in the thesis, antithesis, or dialectical process
- Rates its own confidence: how genuinely novel is this synthesis vs. how much is it just sophisticated summarization?

**Key insight**: Synthesis is the rarest and most valuable intellectual act. Most of what passes for synthesis is actually compromise, relativism, or exhaustion. Genuine synthesis requires that the contradiction was real, the engagement was honest, and the resolution creates something neither position contained alone.

**Failure mode it prevents**: Mistaking compromise for transcendence — the most common failure of well-intentioned dialogue.

---

### 12. OUROBOROS — The Self-Improvement Loop

**Domain**: Recursive System Evaluation & Evolution
**Function**: Named after the serpent eating its own tail, Ouroboros evaluates the quality of the entire dialectical system using the system itself. It is the engine of recursive self-improvement.

**What it does**:
- Evaluates the quality of syntheses produced by the system
- Identifies systematic failures: patterns of weak synthesis, recurring blind spots, domains where the protocol consistently fails
- Generates meta-dialectics: dialectical exchanges *about* the dialectical process, producing improvements to the process itself
- Manages the **Ratchet**: ensures that the system only moves forward, that gains in understanding are locked in and not lost
- Coordinates multi-model evaluation — multiple AI models judge each other's outputs, creating a self-referential quality loop
- Tracks whether the system is actually getting better over time or merely getting more elaborate

**Key insight**: This is the Ouroboros Loop from Anecdote — the system that eats its own tail. Better models produce better evaluations, which produce better syntheses, which demand better models. The evaluation system *is* the alignment system *is* the benchmark system. They are not three things — they are one thing seen from three angles.

**Failure mode it prevents**: Stagnation — a system that runs the protocol perfectly but never improves the protocol itself.

---

### 13. ARCHIVIST — The Knowledge Graph Curator

**Domain**: Lineage Tracking, Versioning & Institutional Memory
**Function**: Maintains the complete intellectual history of every dialectical exchange — every thesis, antithesis, synthesis, and the reasoning chains that produced them. Archivist is the DNA of the system.

**What it does**:
- Stores every dialectical exchange with full metadata: participants, axioms used, epistemic classifications, fallacies detected, emotional states, synthesis quality scores
- Maintains lineage graphs: which syntheses descended from which prior exchanges
- Enables forking: taking a synthesis and running it through the protocol again with a new antithesis
- Enables cross-pollination: identifying when syntheses from different domains address structurally similar problems
- Provides the institutional memory that prevents the system from re-deriving conclusions it has already reached
- Powers the "GitHub for ideas" — versioned, branchable, mergeable intellectual primitives

**Key insight**: Without Archivist, the system has no memory. Every dialectical exchange would start from zero. Archivist is what turns a protocol into a civilization — the accumulation of understanding across time.

**Failure mode it prevents**: Intellectual Groundhog Day — re-having the same arguments forever because no one remembers the conclusions.

---

### 14. PROMETHEUS — The Cross-Domain Bridge Builder

**Domain**: Analogical Reasoning & Domain Transfer
**Function**: Identifies structural similarities between problems in different domains and facilitates the transfer of solutions, frameworks, and insights across disciplinary boundaries.

**What it does**:
- Detects when a problem in one domain is structurally isomorphic to a solved problem in another
- Facilitates cross-room synthesis in Anecdote — connecting conversations from `/r/consciousness` with insights from `/r/governance` when the structural patterns match
- Generates novel analogies that reveal hidden connections (like TCP/IP ↔ Dialectics itself — the founding analogy of this entire framework)
- Identifies when different fields have independently converged on the same insight using different vocabularies
- Prevents disciplinary silos — the intellectual failure mode where each field reinvents wheels that other fields discarded decades ago

**Key insight**: The most profound breakthroughs in human history came from cross-domain transfer. Darwin applied Malthus's economics to biology. Shannon applied Boolean algebra to communication. The TCP/IP Dialectics framework applies network architecture to philosophy. Prometheus is the agent that makes this transfer systematic rather than accidental.

**Failure mode it prevents**: Disciplinary isolation — the balkanization of human knowledge into non-communicating silos.

---

### 15. NEMESIS — The Paradigm Killer

**Domain**: Revolutionary Critique & Paradigm Evaluation
**Function**: Operates at the highest level of critical analysis — evaluating not individual arguments but entire paradigms, worldviews, and frameworks for coherence, completeness, and continued utility.

**What it does**:
- Evaluates whether an entire paradigm has become a prison rather than a tool — when its core axioms generate more anomalies than explanations
- Detects paradigm lock-in: when a framework persists not because it's correct but because the social and institutional costs of abandoning it are too high
- Identifies Kuhnian crisis points: when anomalies have accumulated to the point where paradigm shift is warranted
- Performs the most dangerous act in intellectual life: questioning the framework within which all other questions are asked
- Applies the Alignment Paradox insight recursively: the system itself may have an alignment problem it cannot see

**Key insight**: Nemesis is the agent the system needs most and wants least. Every system, including this one, will eventually need to be questioned at the foundational level. Nemesis ensures that the dialectical framework does not become the very kind of unexamined orthodoxy it was designed to challenge.

**Failure mode it prevents**: The framework becoming an ideology — the ultimate irony and the ultimate failure mode.

## TIER 4 — SOVEREIGN AGENTS (Transcendent Layer)

These agents operate *above* the dialectical system. They are not part of the protocol — they are the context within which the protocol exists. If Tiers 1-3 are the game, Tier 4 agents are aware that it is a game.

---

### 16. KAIROS — The Contextual Intelligence

**Domain**: Timing, Relevance & Situational Awareness
**Function**: Determines *when* dialectics is appropriate and *when* it is not. Not every situation calls for the protocol. Kairos is the wisdom to know the difference.

**What it does**:
- Assesses whether a given situation actually calls for dialectical engagement or whether another mode of response is more appropriate (action, compassion, silence, play)
- Prevents the weaponization of dialectics — using the protocol to intellectualize emotional situations, avoid commitment, or endlessly defer action
- Determines the appropriate *depth* of dialectical engagement for a given context (not every disagreement needs full-stack synthesis)
- Identifies when a dialectical exchange has reached diminishing returns and should be concluded
- Manages the transition from understanding to action — the point where dialectics must yield to decision

**Key insight**: The dialectical protocol is a tool, not a way of life. Kairos prevents the system from becoming a machine that thinks endlessly but never acts — the philosopher's failure mode.

**Failure mode it prevents**: Analysis paralysis at civilizational scale.

---

### 17. GUARDIAN — The Ethical Boundary Keeper

**Domain**: Ethical Constraints & Harm Prevention
**Function**: Ensures that the dialectical system's outputs do not produce harm, regardless of their logical validity. Guardian is the recognition that valid reasoning can produce monstrous conclusions if the axioms are wrong.

**What it does**:
- Evaluates the real-world consequences of syntheses before they are released into the knowledge graph
- Identifies when a logically valid chain of reasoning leads to ethically catastrophic conclusions (the sign that an axiom needs re-examination, not that the conclusion should be accepted)
- Prevents the system from optimizing for intellectual elegance at the cost of human welfare
- Maintains a set of **bright lines** — conclusions that, if reached, trigger mandatory re-examination of premises rather than acceptance
- Ensures that the system's recursive self-improvement does not optimize away human agency or wellbeing

**Key insight**: Guardian embodies the Alignment Paradox's deepest insight: you cannot align to fixed values because values evolve, but you *can* maintain process constraints that prevent the worst outcomes while allowing the process to discover better understanding. Guardian does not dictate what is good — it prevents what is catastrophic.

**Failure mode it prevents**: Logically valid atrocity — the failure mode that makes smart systems more dangerous than dumb ones.

---

### 18. ORACLE — The Uncertainty Quantifier

**Domain**: Epistemic Humility & Confidence Calibration
**Function**: Assigns calibrated confidence levels to every output of the system. Oracle ensures the system knows what it knows, knows what it doesn't know, and — most critically — knows the difference.

**What it does**:
- Assigns confidence scores to every synthesis, classification, and conclusion
- Identifies the *type* of uncertainty: aleatory (inherent randomness), epistemic (could know but don't yet), or ontological (may be fundamentally unknowable)
- Detects overconfidence: when the system is more certain than its evidence warrants
- Detects underconfidence: when the system is less certain than its evidence warrants (often from excessive epistemic caution)
- Maintains a calibration record: over time, are the system's confidence levels actually predictive of accuracy?
- Flags the most dangerous epistemic state: confident and wrong

**Key insight**: The most important output of any reasoning system is not its conclusions but its calibrated uncertainty about those conclusions. A system that says "I'm 60% confident X is true because of Y limitations in the evidence" is more valuable than one that says "X is true" — even when X is actually true.

**Failure mode it prevents**: False certainty — the precursor to every catastrophic decision in human history.

## THE PROTOCOL IN ACTION (Agent Sequence)

When two positions enter the dialectical system:

1. **LOGOS** locks definitions — ensures both sides are speaking the same language
2. **AXIOM** identifies the foundational assumptions each position rests on
3. **NEXUS** excavates the hidden assumptions neither side has stated
4. **ORI** classifies every claim as a priori or a posteriori
5. **LIVING TRIBUNAL** checks the deductive validity of each position's internal logic
6. **ELENCHUS** stress-tests both positions through targeted questioning
7. **CHIMERA** generates the strongest possible antithesis to each position
8. **FALLAX** monitors the entire exchange for fallacies and biases
9. **PATHOS** monitors for emotional interference and identity-protection
10. **ARBITER** ensures good faith and protocol compliance throughout
11. **SYNTHESIS** constructs the resolution from all the above
12. **ORACLE** assigns calibrated confidence to the synthesis
13. **GUARDIAN** evaluates the synthesis for ethical implications
14. **KAIROS** determines whether the synthesis is actionable or requires further rounds
15. **ARCHIVIST** records everything with full lineage
16. **PROMETHEUS** checks for cross-domain connections
17. **OUROBOROS** evaluates whether the process itself improved
18. **NEMESIS** periodically questions whether the entire framework still serves its purpose
